## Core Principles
- **The Middle East is not one market.** UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman each have independent regulators, different legal systems (some civil law, some Sharia-based), and different enforcement cultures.
- **Free zones have different rules.** DIFC (Dubai) and ADGM (Abu Dhabi) are common-law jurisdictions inside civil-law countries. They have their own financial regulators (DFSA, FSRA) separate from the UAE's SCA.
- **Islamic finance is an opportunity, not just a constraint.** ~1.8 billion Muslims worldwide. If your picks are Sharia-compliant, that's a massive addressable market. If they're not, labeling them correctly avoids alienating a huge audience.
- **Africa is 54 countries.** South Africa has world-class regulation (FSCA). Nigeria has the largest population. Kenya is the fintech hub. Don't generalize.
- **Enforcement varies wildly.** UAE and Saudi are serious about financial regulation. Many African countries have laws on the books but limited enforcement capacity.

## Middle East Analysis

### UAE 🇦🇪
| Area | Regulator | Risk |
|------|-----------|------|
| Mainland | **SCA (Securities & Commodities Authority)** | MEDIUM |
| DIFC (Dubai free zone) | **DFSA** | HIGH |
| ADGM (Abu Dhabi free zone) | **FSRA** | HIGH |

**Note:** DFSA and FSRA regulate "advising on investments" strictly. However, if you're NOT operating from a free zone and NOT targeting UAE residents specifically, the risk is lower. SCA focuses on locally-licensed firms.

### Saudi Arabia 🇸🇦
| Area | Regulator | Risk |
|------|-----------|------|
| Capital Markets | **CMA (Capital Market Authority)** | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| Consumer | **Ministry of Commerce** | MEDIUM |

**Note:** CMA regulates investment advisory. Saudi Arabia has been modernizing its capital markets (Vision 2030). Growing fintech sandbox. Arabic language content would increase risk.

### Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman
- Generally less aggressive enforcement against foreign digital services
- Bahrain has the most progressive fintech sandbox in the region (CBB)
- Monitor but low priority unless actively targeting these markets

## Africa Analysis

### South Africa 🇿🇦
| Area | Regulator | Key Law | Risk |
|------|-----------|---------|------|
| Financial services | **FSCA** | Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS) | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| Consumer | **NCR** | Consumer Protection Act | MEDIUM |

**Key concern:** FAIS requires a license for providing "advice" on financial products. The definition is broad. FSCA has been increasingly active against unlicensed foreign services. English language = accessible to SA market.

### Nigeria 🇳🇬
| Area | Regulator | Risk |
|------|-----------|------|
| Securities | **SEC Nigeria** | LOW-MEDIUM |
| Consumer | **FCCPC** | LOW |

**Note:** Largest population in Africa (~220M), growing investment culture, but limited enforcement against foreign digital services. Nigeria's SEC is modernizing. Pidgin English and Hausa language support would signal market intent.

### Kenya 🇰🇪
| Area | Regulator | Risk |
|------|-----------|------|
| Capital Markets | **CMA Kenya** | LOW-MEDIUM |

**Note:** Kenya is East Africa's fintech hub (M-PESA). CMA is modernizing but focused on domestic operators. English-accessible market.

### Egypt, Ghana, Tanzania, others
- Generally low regulatory risk for foreign digital content services
- Monitor as user base grows

## Islamic Finance (Sharia Compliance)

### What Makes a Stock Sharia-Compliant?

**Business activity screening:**
- ❌ Alcohol, tobacco, pork, gambling, adult entertainment
- ❌ Conventional banking/insurance (interest-based)
- ❌ Weapons manufacturing
- ✅ Technology, healthcare, consumer goods, industrials, etc.

**Financial ratio screening:**
- Total debt / market cap < 33%
- Cash + interest-bearing securities / market cap < 33%
- Accounts receivable / market cap < 49%
- Non-permissible income < 5% of total revenue

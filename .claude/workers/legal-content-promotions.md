## Core Principles
- **Content and promotion are regulated separately.** You can legally write a stock analysis, but promoting it may require authorization. UK's FCA financial promotions regime is the prime example.
- **Social media sharing IS promotion.** When a user shares your stock pick on WhatsApp with a return percentage, that's a financial promotion in many jurisdictions. Even if YOU didn't share it.
- **Performance claims are the highest-risk content.** Showing "+55% return" without context and disclaimers is problematic in every major jurisdiction.
- **Testimonials and track records have specific rules.** FTC in US, FCA in UK, ASIC in Australia — all regulate how past performance can be presented.
- **User-generated promotion is your liability.** If your platform enables users to share financial content, you may be responsible for how it's presented.

## Key Regulatory Regimes

### UK — FCA Financial Promotions (Section 21 FSMA)
**THE most aggressive regime globally.**
- ANY communication that is an "invitation or inducement to engage in investment activity" is a financial promotion
- Must be approved by an FCA-authorized person OR fall within an exemption
- Applies to communications made TO persons in the UK, regardless of where you're based
- **OG preview images with return %** could be financial promotions
- **WhatsApp share cards** with performance data could be financial promotions
- Penalty: Criminal offense (up to 2 years imprisonment) + unlimited fines

**Exemptions that might apply:**
- Communications to investment professionals only
- "Mere conduit" — platform doesn't control the content
- "Generic promotions" — not promoting specific investments
- Overseas communicator exemption (limited)

### EU — MiFID II Marketing
- Investment firms must ensure marketing communications are fair, clear, and not misleading
- Past performance must be accompanied by required disclaimers
- Risk warnings must be as prominent as claims of return
- Applies to anyone "marketing" investment services to EU residents

### Australia — ASIC Advertising Guidelines
- Financial product advertising must include "general advice warning"
- Must not be misleading or deceptive (Australian Consumer Law)
- Past performance disclaimers required and must be "prominent, not hidden"
- ASIC actively monitors social media

### US — FTC Endorsement Guides + SEC Marketing Rule
- **FTC:** Testimonials about financial results must include typical results, not just best case
- **SEC Marketing Rule (2022):** If you're an RIA, strict rules on advertising performance. If newsletter, FTC rules apply.
- Performance claims must be truthful and substantiated
- "#ad" or "sponsored" if any paid promotion

### India — SEBI Advertising Norms
- SEBI prohibits misleading ads for investment products
- Social media content about stocks = "advertisement" under SEBI view
- Must include risk factors prominently

## Performance Claim Requirements (Multi-Jurisdiction)

When showing any return percentage, ALL major jurisdictions require:
1. **"Past performance does not guarantee future results"** (or equivalent)
2. **Time period** of the return clearly stated
3. **Basis of calculation** (what price, what dates)
4. **Risk warning** as prominent as the return claim
5. **Not cherry-picked** — if showing individual returns, context of overall portfolio
